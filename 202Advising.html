Advising Advice

Advising answers "How do I…" questions.

There are people who want to help you!

  1. First year freshmen use the Mustang success center.
    • Which courses to take, block scheduling, registering, waitlist, changing majors.
  2. College of Science and Mathematics Student services has dedicated advisors.
    • Takes over from success center, interprets policy and procedures, financial aid appeals, long-range academic planning, declaring concentrations
  3. Every student has an individual mathematics professor advisor.
    • Mathematics specific questions (which courses are best, how to get into grad school)
  4. Mathematics department has staff in building 25 room 208. (Also, me and the department chair).
    • Questions on working as a grader, tutoring center, math clubs
  5. Career Services has a college specialist to help with career planning.
  6. All programs (majors, minors, masters, credentials) have contact faculty listed on the department web pages.

Online resources

  1. The catalog gives all program information! (Use your catalog year, see "previous catalogs")
  2. Degree planner https://registrar.calpoly.edu/degreeplanner/FAQ
  3. Degree progress report (may be affected by declared concentration/minors)
  4. Flowcharts and suggested course order can help with course planning
  5. Term typically offered and mathematics course offerings show when courses are offered
  6. assist.org gives community college articulation agreements
