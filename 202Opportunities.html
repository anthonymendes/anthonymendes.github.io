Opportunity is knocking!

There are many outstanding opportunities available to mathematics majors. Please take advantage of them!

Academics

  1. Minoring with a mathematics major is a great boost to your degree. These popular minors for mathematics majors are excellent choices.
  2. The Cal Poly math department competes nationally in the Putnam Competition (led by Dr. Morgan Sherman) and Math Modeling Competition (led by Dr. Dave Camp). We offer 1 unit courses in Fall quarter to prepare for the competitions.
  3. Be aware the mathematics department will usually support student travel to professional conferences, especially to present any research that you have done with a faculty member.
  4. The mathematics department occasionally emails information about colloquiums. These are one hour lectures on an advanced topic, usually by a professor from another university. They can be a nice window to the world of research mathematics for students interested in graduate school.

Student Employment

Mathematics student employment opportunities include:

  1. tutoring in the math help hub in the Writing and Learning Center,
  2. working as a mathematics workshop or study session leader with the Academic Skills Center,
  3. becoming an upper-division mathematics department workshop leader (contact the department after earning an A in a course such as MATH 306 or MATH 412), and
  4. working as a homework grader (inquire with a professor with whom you have a good relationship).

Summertime

Excellent uses of your summer include:

  1. The Frost summer research program where you are paid to work closely with a faculty member on cutting edge research. This special program is only available to Cal Poly students!
  2. The National Science Foundation funds the Research Experiences for Undergraduates (REU) program. This is a highly competitive nationwide program where you are given a stipend to go to another university to conduct mathematics research. This looks great on applications to Ph.D. programs, so plan ahead an apply early!
  3. Many companies have summer internships for mathematics majors. The job fair and Cal Poly Career Services can help you find an internship at an interesting organization. Many successful internships have translated into job offers!

Fun with friends!

  1. Contrary to the stereotype, math majors are social! We have two clubs for having fun as a mathematics major: the Math Club and the Association for Women in Mathematics. The Math Club hosts annual events such as faculty-student poker tournaments, softball games, and barbecues.
  2. Study for your classes with other math majors (or just goof off!) in the private mathematics student lounge in the Baker science center.

Career advice

Career advice for mathematics majors is provided by the American Mathematical Society, the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics, and the American Statistical Association.
