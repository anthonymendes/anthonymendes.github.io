Blue Book Exercises

Blue book exercises will be updated here after they are given in class. The blue books will be collected at the final exam.

  1. Solve \(\begin{cases} x + y + z = 0, \\ x - y + z = 0, \\ y + 2 z = 1. \end{cases}\)
  2. Put this system into REF and then state if there is no solution, one solution, or an infinite number of solutions: \(\begin{cases} x - y + z = 2, \\ 2 x - z = 0, \\ 2y - 3 z = -4. \end{cases}\)
  3. Is \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}\) a linear combination of \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \\ -2 \end{bmatrix}\)?
  4. Is the span of \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 0 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix}\), \(\begin{bmatrix} 4 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix}\) equal to \(\mathbb{R}^3\).
  5. a. Calculate \(\begin{bmatrix} 4 & 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 0 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ x_3 \end{bmatrix}\). b. Write \(\begin{bmatrix} -x_1 + 2 x_3 \\ x_2 + x_3 \\ x_3\end{bmatrix}\) as a matrix multiplication of the form \(A \mathbf{x}\).
  6. Write the solutions to \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 2 & 4 \\ -1 & 2 & -1 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}\) as the span of some vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^4\).
  7. Are the columns of \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 1 \\ 1 & 4 & 0 \\ -2 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\) linearly independent?
  8. Let \(A = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\). Draw "before and after" pictures of the images of the vectors \(\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(\begin{bmatrix} -\sqrt{2} \\ \sqrt{2} \end{bmatrix}\) after multiplying by \(A\). Can you describe what \(A\) does to vectors?
  9. Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 1 \\ 2 & 0 & 3 \\ 1 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\), \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\). Calculate \(A B\) and \(B A\).
  10. Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\) and \(B = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\).
  11. If possible, find the inverse to \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\).
  12. Calculate the determinant of \(\begin{bmatrix}1 & 2 & 3 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 & 2 \\ 1 & 0 & 3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\).
  13. What is the determinant of an upper triangular matrix?
  14. Let \(V\) be the vector space \(\mathbb{R}^3\) and \(S\) be the set of vectors of the form \(\begin{bmatrix} a \\ b \\ c \end{bmatrix}\) with \(a + 2 b + c =0\). Is \(S\) a vector space. Repeat if \(S\) is now vectors that satisfy \(a \geq 0\).
  15. Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 5 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\). Write the nullspace of \(A\) as the span of some vectors.
  16. \(1,x+1,x^2+x+1\) is a basis for \(P_2\). Write \(2x^2 -1\) in this basis.
  17. Find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors for \(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 2 & 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\).
  18. Let \(A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 0 \\ 2 & -1 \end{bmatrix}\). Find the eigenvalue and eigenvectors \(\lambda_1, \mathbf{v}_1\) and \(\lambda_2, \mathbf{v}_2\) for \(A\). Then let \(P = [ \mathbf{v}_1 \mathbf{v}_2 ]\). Find \(P^{-1}\) and calculate find \(P^{-1} A P\).
