Course content

Topics include graph isomorphism, vertex colorings, trees, planar graphs, Eulerian and Hamiltonian paths, connectivity, matchings, the adjacency matrix, and the Laplacian.

The prerequisite for this course is MATH 248 or junior standing. At a minimum, students should have had exposure to basic proof techniques (such as proof by contradiction and proof by induction).

Some topics use concepts from matrix algebra (such as eigenvalues and eigenvectors, diagonalization, and properties of real symmetric matrices). There may be a brief introduction to some of the needed concepts, but a basic familiarity with matrices is assumed.

Warning: This is a rigorous course that contains challenging topics and exercises. Students who have never taken a 300+ level mathematics course are advised to form study groups.

Exercise Sets

The exercise sets are important and high quality work is expected. You will not receive credit for an exercise unless careful explanations are included. Please turn in assignments of a quality appropriate for a professional workplace.

Please get started on the exercises early! To keep everyone on track, late assignments will be penalized 20% per day late. If life issues get in the way, please let me know before the assignment is due so that we can make special arrangements.

Follow these steps when turning in the assignments and exams:

  1. Neatly and clearly write out the solutions by hand. This may require writing rough drafts; please do not submit messy scratch work.
  2. It is difficult to read and grade .jpg image files, so please create a single .pdf file from your exercise sets using a document scanner phone application.
  3. Upload the file to in our Canvas site before the due date.

If you are using a tablet to write out solutions or know mathematical typesetting (LaTeX) or have any other method of creating legible solutions in a .pdf file, then of course this can be used instead of handwritten solutions.
