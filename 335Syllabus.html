Course content

I have been greatly looking forward to sharing the ideas in this course with you!

Topics include graph isomorphism, vertex colorings, trees, planar graphs, Eulerian and Hamiltonian paths, connectivity, matchings, the adjacency matrix, and the Laplacian. The official department course outline contains more details.

The prerequisite for this course is MATH 248 or junior standing. At a minimum, students should have had exposure to basic proof techniques (such as proof by contradiction and proof by induction). Some topics use concepts from matrix algebra (such as eigenvalues and eigenvectors, diagonalization, and properties of real symmetric matrices). There may be a brief introduction to some of the needed concepts, but a basic familiarity with matrices is assumed. Please be ready to look these topics up and learn them on your own (with my help in office hours if needed) if necessary.

Warning: This is a rigorous course that contains challenging topics and exercises. Students who have never taken a 300+ level mathematics course are advised to form study groups.

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to

  1. Determine whether or not two given graphs are isomorphic.
  2. Produce bounds on the chromatic number of a graph.
  3. Paraphrase proofs of classical theorems in graph theory.
  4. Demonstrate the non-planarity of specific graphs.
  5. Illustrate graph algorithms on examples.
  6. Use matrix algebra to characterize graph properties.
