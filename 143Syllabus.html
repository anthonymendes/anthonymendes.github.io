The honors bunch!

The honors calculus section covers the same content as the standard calculus sections, but at a slightly faster pace. This gives us a bit of extra time to explore some amazing mathematics that other students will simply never see. If you're intellectually curious and enjoy mathematics, then this is certainly the class for you!

Honors calculus students are not held to a higher standard than students in other Calculus sections, so your grades will not suffer because you have taken an honors section. Honors calculus is part of the Cal Poly honors program, but you do not need to be in the honors program to take my class.

A big benefit in taking honors calculus is the fantastic collegial environment we will create together. You will have outstanding peers and a dedicated, caring professor.

Course content

I have been greatly looking forward to sharing the ideas in this course with you!

The topics are found in chapters 10–13 of our textbook. These are extremely useful topics which find numerous applications in a wide variety of subjects. The official department course outline contains more details.

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to

  1. Classify infinite series as convergent or divergent using standard tests such as the comparison tests, the ratio test, and the root test.
  2. Calculate power series and Taylor series and find their radius of convergence.
  3. Apply the concepts of parametric equations and polar coordinates to problems in Calculus.
  4. Solve problems involving vector algebra and elementary differential vector calculus.

Exercise Sets

The exercise sets are important and high quality work is expected. You will not receive credit for an exercise unless work is included. Please turn in assignments of a quality appropriate for a professional workplace.

Please get started on the exercises early! To keep everyone on track, late assignments will be penalized 20% per day late. If life issues get in the way, please let me know before the assignment is due so that we can make special arrangements.

Follow these steps when turning in the assignments and exams:

  1. Neatly and clearly write out the solutions by hand. This may require writing rough drafts; please do not submit messy scratch work.
  2. It is difficult to read and grade .jpg image files, so please create a single .pdf file from your exercise sets using a document scanner phone application.
  3. Upload the file to in our Canvas site before the due date. If you are using a tablet to write out solutions or know mathematical typesetting (LaTeX) or have any other method of creating legible solutions in a .pdf file, then of course this can be used instead of handwritten solutions.
