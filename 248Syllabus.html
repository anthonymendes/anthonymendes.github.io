Content and learning objectives

I have been greatly looking forward to sharing the ideas in this course with you!

This class is not unlike a foreign language class. Just like Spanish class where you learn to read, write, and understand Spanish, in this course you will read, write, and understand mathematics! There are also some fundamental topics such as sets, equivalence relations, and functions that will be introduced along the way. The official department course outline contains more details on the course content.

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to

  1. demonstrate an understanding of how mathematics is communicated,
  2. determine when logical statements are equivalent,
  3. apply standard techniques (direct proof, contradiction, induction, etc.) in proving theorems,
  4. construct clear and concise proofs of elementary theorems, and
  5. use fundamental mathematical objects (such as set, relation, function) in logical arguments.

Exercise Sets

The exercise sets are important and high quality work is expected. You will not receive credit for an exercise unless careful explanations are included. Please turn in assignments of a quality appropriate for a professional workplace.

Please get started on the exercises early! Material in this course builds on itself---if you fall behind then it will be a challenge for you to catch up. To keep everyone on track, late assignments will be penalized 20% per day late. If life issues get in the way, please let me know before the assignment is due so that we can make special arrangements.

Follow these steps when turning in the assignments and exams:

  1. Neatly and clearly write out the solutions by hand. This may require writing rough drafts; please do not submit messy scratch work.
  2. It is difficult to read and grade .jpg image files, so please create a single .pdf file from your exercise sets using a document scanner phone application.
  3. Upload the file to in our Canvas site before the due date.

If you are using a tablet to write out solutions or know mathematical typesetting (LaTeX) or have any other method of creating legible solutions in a .pdf file, then of course this can be used instead of handwritten solutions.

Exercises will be graded by a teaching assistant and I will grade the exams. The homework grader will randomly select a subset of exercises to grade carefully for accuracy. Please contact me when you have questions about grading.

Exams

There are two midterm exams and one final exam.

  • Midterm 1 covers the content included in the exercises found in Set 1 through Set 5. It can be accessed in Canvas on Jul 9 at your scheduled class time. Solutions should be uploaded to Canvas one hour later in the same way that you have been turning in the exercises.
  • Midterm 2 covers the content included in the exercises found in Set 6 through Set 10. It can be accessed in Canvas on Jul 30 at your scheduled class time.
  • The final exam is cumulative but will emphasize the topics not covered on the midterms. It can be accessed in Canvas on Aug 13 at your scheduled class time.

Please refer to the Academic Honesty section below for which resources are permitted on exams.

Academic honesty

The nature of virtual classes may tempt some students into committing an act of academic dishonesty. Please take pride in yourself and your work, practice integrity, and respect the efforts of your classmates and your professor.

The table below clarifies which resources are available for use on exercise sets and exams. A "No" means the resource is not permitted and a "Yes" means that it is permitted.
