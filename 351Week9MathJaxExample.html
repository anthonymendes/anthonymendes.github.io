This web page provides an example of how MathJax can be used to easily typeset mathematics in HTML. Binomial coefficients can be typeset like this: \({\displaystyle 1 + 2 + \cdots + n = \binom{n+1}{2}}\) and matrices can be typeset like this: \begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \qquad \begin{vmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{vmatrix} \qquad \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \qquad \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \qquad \begin{Vmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{Vmatrix} \end{equation*} By convention, matrices are not written in boldface but vectors such as \(\mathbf{x}\) are.

The correct spacing of integrals... \[ \int_{-\infty}^\infty \int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-(x^2+y^2)} \, dx \, dy = \int_0^{2 \pi} \int_0^\infty e^{-r^2} r \, dr \, d\theta. \] An incorrect spacing of integrals... \[ \int_{-\infty}^\infty \int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-(x^2+y^2)} dx dy = \int_0^{2 \pi} \int_0^\infty e^{-r^2} r dr d\theta. \] An example of multiline mathematics... \begin{align*} \int_a^b x^n \, dx & = \left. \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} \right|_a^b \\ & = \frac{b^{n+1}}{n+1} -\frac{a^{n+1}}{n+1} \\ & = \frac{1}{n+1}(b^{n+1}-a^{n+1}). \end{align*} An example of cases... \[ |x| = \begin{cases} -x & \text{if \(x < 0\)}, \\ x & \text{otherwise}. \end{cases} \]
