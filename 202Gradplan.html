Graduation Plan

The assignment is to create a valid graduation plan to guide your remaining time at Cal Poly. This is the most important assignment in Math 202!

Creating the plan

Creating an individually tailored graduation plan can be complicated. Please ensure that

  1. The plan meets all requirements for general education, the major, any concentration and any minors.

    All such requirements are in the catalog. Use the catalog corresponding to your catalog year. This can be found in the Academics tab of the Cal Poly Portal, listed directly under the “Degree Information” subheading.

    General education, major requirements, and concentration requirements are all found on the curriculum sheet in the degree flowcharts & curriculum displays page. Follow this closely!

    The general education comprehensive guide can help with selecting G.E.s.

    When deciding on exactly which courses to take, the informal course descriptions can give you a better idea of what to expect from each course.

  2. Prerequisites are met for each course.

    The suggested order of mathematics courses has the prerequisites for each course. This information is also found in the expanded course outlines.

    See the catalog or the department homepages for prerequisites for non-mathematics courses.

  3. Courses are offered in the quarters that you plan to take them.

    The planned course offerings show when courses are offered. Most upper division mathematics courses are offered once or twice a year, so be careful!

    General education courses are available every quarter, although popular courses fill up quickly. If completing a minor, visit the minor department homepage to find when classes are offered. The registrar also maintains a list of when courses are typically offered.

It may be helpful to print and fill out the graduation plan worksheet when working on the plan, especially in preparation of your meeting with your advisor.

Checking the plan

You are required to review your graduation plan with an academic advisor.

There is a mathematics faculty member who is assigned to be your department advisor. This is your contact person for any issues that arise when completing the undergraduate degree. If you do not know your academic advisor, please email me (aamendes@calpoly.edu). The college student services department can also help review your plan.

The mathematics faculty greatly enjoy meeting with students and helping them plan for the future! However, faculty are also busy with classes and research, so please do not wait until the last minute get in contact.

When meeting with your advisor, please know the answers to these questions:

  1. Which courses do you plan to take in order to meet all requirements for general education, the major, any concentration and any minors?
  2. How did you check to see if courses are offered when you want to take them?
  3. Did you verify that you will meet the prerequisites for each course?

Formatting and submitting the plan

Format the plan following the template shown in this example: 

Name: Musty Mustang
Catalog: 2020-2021
Concentration: General
Minors: Statistics
Checked by: Prof. Mathy Mustang
Date checked: October 12, 2020
Incoming credit:
(MATH 141 MATH 142)
Graduation Plan:
(Fall 2018 MATH 143 PHYS 141 TH 210)
(Winter 2019 PHYS 133 MATH 241 STAT 301 MATH 206)
(Spring 2019 MATH 242 BIO 111 MU 101 ENGL 145)

(Fall 2019 MATH 248 HIST 213 COMS 102 CSC 101)
(Winter 2020 HIST 337 MATH 306 ASTR 324 PHIL 231)
(Spring 2020 ENGL 251 MATH 406 MATH 202 MATH 341)
(Summer 2020 NR 324)

(Fall 2020 MATH 412 MATH 481 STAT 305)
(Winter 2021 MATH 413 MATH 440 STAT 302)
(Spring 2021 MATH 414 MATH 482 MATH 459)

(Fall 2021 MATH 335 STAT 425 MATH 336 MATH 408)
(Winter 2022 MATH 451 MATH 409 STAT 334)
(Spring 2022 STAT 419 PHIL 310 MATH 483)

Include lines with your name, catalog year, concentration, minors, and who checked your plan and when. Then, to help me run the plan through the software that I have written, make sure that:

  1. The plan is submitted as plain text (for example, not in a word document or .pdf file). This can be done by copy/pasting the text into the submission box in Canvas.
  2. So prerequisites can be checked,
    • include a line after Incoming credit: that includes any courses that you have credit for (from AP exams, community college courses, etc.). Format this line in the same way as shown in the example, using the course prefix in all capital letters (such as MATH) and then the course number, separating terms by spaces, and enclosing everything in parenthesis.
    • In your graduation plan section, please include courses from previous quarters that you have passed.
  3. Format each quarter listed in your plan on a new line that
    • starts with a left parenthesis (,
    • followed by one of Fall, Winter, Spring, or Summer and then the year,
    • followed by a list of courses written using the course prefix in all capital letters (such as MATH) and then the course number. Separate everything on the line with spaces (not dashes or commas).
    • End the line using a right parenthesis ).

Submit the plan in Canvas on or before the due date. Credit for this assignment will only be given for creating a valid graduation plan.

Disclaimer: I will do my best to find errors in your plan, but my checks should not be considered an official guarantee that the plan is correct.
