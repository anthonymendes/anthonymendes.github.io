Graduation Plan

The assignment is to create a valid graduation plan to guide your remaining time at Cal Poly. This is the most important assignment in Math 202!

Creating the plan

Creating an individually tailored graduation plan can be complicated. Please ensure that

  1. The plan meets all requirements for general education, the major, any concentration and any minors.

    All such requirements are in the catalog. The degree flowcharts & curriculum displays and the general education comprehensive guide can help. See the degree progress report in the Portal to see which G.E. courses you still need to complete after AP and transfer credits are considered.

  2. Prerequisites are met for each course.

    The suggested order of mathematics courses has the prerequisites for each course. This information is also found in the expanded course outlines. See the catalog or the department homepages for prerequisites for non-mathematics courses.

  3. Courses are offered in the quarters that you plan to take them.

    Most upper division mathematics courses are only offered once or twice a year.

Formatting the plan

Format the plan following the template shown in this example: 

Name: Musty Mustang
Student ID: 012345678
Catalog: 2022-2026
Concentration: General
Minors: Statistics
Incoming credit:
(MATH 141 MATH 142)
Graduation Plan:
(Fall 2024 MATH 143 PHYS 141 TH 210)
(Winter 2025 PHYS 133 MATH 241 STAT 301 MATH 206)
(Spring 2025 MATH 242 BIO 111 MU 101 ENGL 145)

(Fall 2025 MATH 248 HIST 213 COMS 102 CSC 101)
(Winter 2026 HIST 337 MATH 306 ASTR 324 PHIL 231)
(Spring 2026 ENGL 251 MATH 406 MATH 202 MATH 341)
(Summer 2026 NR 324)

(Fall 2025 MATH 412 MATH 481 STAT 305)
(Winter 2026 MATH 413 MATH 440 STAT 302)
(Spring 2026 MATH 414 MATH 482 MATH 459)

(Fall 2026 MATH 335 STAT 425 MATH 336 MATH 408)
(Spring 2026 STAT 419 PHIL 310 MATH 483)

Include lines with your name, student ID, catalog year, concentration, and any minors. To help me run the plan through the software that I have written, make sure that:

  1. The plan is submitted as plain text (for example, not in a word document or .pdf file). This can be done by copy/pasting the text into the submission box in Canvas.
  2. So prerequisites can be checked,
    • include a line after Incoming credit: that includes any courses that you have credit for (from AP exams, community college courses, etc.). Format this line in the same way as shown in the example, using the course prefix in all capital letters (such as MATH) and then the course number, separating terms by spaces, and enclosing everything in parenthesis.
    • In your graduation plan section, please include courses from previous quarters that you have passed.
  3. Format each quarter listed in your plan on a new line that
    • starts with a left parenthesis (,
    • followed by one of Fall, Winter, Spring, or Summer and then the year,
    • followed by a list of courses written using the course prefix in all capital letters (such as MATH) and then the course number. Separate everything on the line with spaces (not dashes or commas).
    • End the line using a right parenthesis ).

Submitting the plan

Submit your properly formatted plan on Canvas. If you make any mistake at all, then you can come to one of the "In-class advising" days on our class calendar and get your plan checked by me during our Math 202 class period. After your plan successfully passes my checks you will receive credit for this assignment.

Disclaimer: I will do my best to find errors in your plan, but my checks should not be considered an official guarantee that the plan is correct.
