Our textbook is Differential Equations and Linear Algebra, 4th edition by Goode and Inning. This is the same text as the prerequisite course (Cal Poly Math 244). We will not be following the text at all times as no single text contains all of the content in our course.

The text will be used as a secondary reference only. It will be cross-referenced with the lessons on our web page. Exercise sets will not come from the text. Therefore, if textbook cost is an issue, it may be possible to squeak by without a copy. However, if you struggle in mathematics classes, be sure to read the text!

Course content

This course introduces linear methods with applications to the solution of differential equations and partial differential equations. Topics include Laplace transforms, Series solutions to ordinary differential equations, Orthogonality, the Gram-Schmidt procedure, least squares, orthogonal bases in function spaces, Sturm-Liouville theory, and Fourier series. The official department course outline contains details on the course content.

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to

  1. Apply the technique of Laplace transforms to applications in ordinary differential equations and linear systems,
  2. Construct series solutions to linear differential equations,
  3. Apply the concept of orthogonality in a variety of applications, including regression and classification, and
  4. Construct solutions to boundary value problems using Fourier Series and Fourier transforms.

Exercise Sets

The exercise sets are important and high quality work is expected. You will not receive credit for an exercise unless work is included. Please turn in assignments of a quality appropriate for a professional workplace.

Follow these steps when turning in the assignments and exams:

  1. Neatly and clearly write out the solutions by hand. This may require writing rough drafts; please do not submit messy scratch work. If you are using a tablet to write out solutions or know mathematical typesetting (LaTeX) or have any other method of creating legible solutions in a .pdf file, then of course this can be used instead of handwritten solutions.
  2. It is difficult to read and grade .jpg image files, so please create a single .pdf file from your exercise sets using a document scanner phone application.
  3. Upload the file to in our Canvas site before the due date.

Each exercise set is out of a total of 10 possible points. For each exercise set three problems are to be randomly selected to be graded on a 0,1,2 scale.

  • A score of 2 means you perfectly solved the problem and clearly showed your solution.
  • A score of 1 means there was a very small error (like a single sign error at the very end of the problem) or the problem wasn't perfectly explained.
  • A score of 0 is assigned to all other issues.

Then the remaining 4 points out of 10 are assigned if all of the homework is completed on time with good effort. If there are two or three missing exercises, expect a score of 0 out of 4.

This grading scheme is designed to promote high quality homework solutions, penalizing mediocre quality and incomplete work.

Please get started on the exercises early! If life issues get in the way, please let me know before the assignment is due so that we can make special arrangements.
