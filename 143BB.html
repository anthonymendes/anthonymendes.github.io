Blue Book Exercises

Blue book exercises will be updated here after they are given in class. These exercises will be collected on the last day of class.

  1. Find the degree \(3\) Taylor polynomial for \(\ln (1 + x)\) at \(x = 0\).
  2. Find the degree \(n\) Taylor polynomial for \(1/(3 + x)\) at \(x = 0\).
  3. The degree \(5\) Taylor polynomial for \(\sin x\) is \(x - x^3/3! + x^5/5!\). Find a bound on the error when using this to approximate \(\sin 2\).
  4. Find the degree \(3\) Taylor polynomial for \(\sqrt{x}\) at \(x = 2\).
  5. Use the formula for the geometric series to simplify these sums:
    • \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty (-1)^n \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{n+1}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{2^{n+2}}{4^n}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (-1)^n\)
  6. Use the integral test to decide if \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}\) converges.
  7. Do these converge?
    • \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{\sqrt{n}-1}{n^2 + 1}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{2^n}{3^n + n}\)
  8. Do these converge?
    • \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{\sqrt{n+1}}{n^3}\)
    • \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{n^2+3n}{n^3 + 2}\)
  9. Does \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{n 2^n}{(n-1)!}\)
  10. For which values of \(x\) does \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \frac{x^{2n}}{n^n}\) converge?
  11. Find the interval of convergence and radius of convergence for the power series \(\displaystyle \sum_{n = 0}^\infty n! x^n\).
  12. Find the series, interval of convergence and radius of convergence for \(x \sin (x^2)\).
  13. Plot \(\begin{cases} x = \sqrt{t} + 1 \\ y = t + 1 \end{cases}\) for \(t \in [0,1]\).
  14. Find the equation of the line tangent to \(\begin{cases} x = 2 t - \pi \sin t \\ y = 2 - \pi \cos t \end{cases}\) at \(t = \pi/2\).
  15. For which values of \(t\) is \(\begin{cases} x = t^2/2 \\ y = t^3 - t^4 \end{cases}\) for \(t \in (0,\infty)\) concave up?
  16. Find the arclength of \(\begin{cases} x = 1 + e^{-t} \\ y = 2 e^{-t} \end{cases}\) for \(t \in [0,1]\).
  17. Find the first positive \(\alpha\) such that the graph of the polar equation \(r(\theta) = \sin \theta\) has a vertical tangent line at \(\theta = \alpha\).
  18. Find the area bounded by the polar curve \(r = 2 \cos (2 \theta)\).
  19. Find the area in the first quadrant bounded by the polar equations \(r = \srqt{\theta}\) and \(r = \theta\).
  20. Find a vector of length \(2\) in the opposite direction of \(\langle 3,4 \rangle\).
  21. Find two unit vectors orthogonal to \(\langle 1,1,1 \rangle\) and \(\langle 2,1,0 \rangle\).
  22. Find the line containing \((2,0,1)\) and perpendicular to both \(\langle 1,1,0 \rangle\) and \(\langle 0,0,2 \rangle\).
